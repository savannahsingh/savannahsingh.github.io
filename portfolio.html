Artist Statement

My art is about telling a story.

I have been inspired by games, cartoons, and comics as a child. Those stories stuck with me as I grew up and became the reason I chose to pursue a career in art. I aim to create a variety of art that can reach out to wider audiences, including children. I take a playful approach to serious topics.

I am very passionate about the environment and often incorporate how humanity is affecting the planet into my pieces. I strive to create art that can be interpreted in more than one way; I believe my environmental works accomplish that. Even when I am not creating environmental works, I strive to create work about sustainability and resilience.

"The Trash Reef"

Digital Illustration, 2019

Made in Clip Studio Paint and PhotoShop

A recent study has shown that 100% of sea turtles in the ocean have plastics or mircoplastics in their bodies. The study examined every species of sea turtle from all over the world. This illustration is based off this statistic. The plants and other sea creatures are substituted by plastics and other pollution that is commonly found in our oceans to show how easy it is for turtles and other animals to confuse the plastic in the oceans with the food they normally eat.

Source: Microplastics found in gut of every sea turtle in new study

"Nature Glitch"

Glitch Art, 2019

Made with Audacity

Pictures that are shared on the internet are often edited to be more appealing to its viewers and this changes people's perceptions of these places or things that are being portrayed. They can make places more interesting and a lot of travel websites take advantage of this to persuade people to visit to these places. I chose a picture of nature specifically because I find nature beautiful without any editing or filter yet people continue to alter images of natural places.
