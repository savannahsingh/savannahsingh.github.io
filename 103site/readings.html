Reading 1

A system is composed of groups that each have a purpose to make all of the groups work together to form a whole. A system is not a single piece working on its own, it has to have multiple parts working together. Modularity is creating individual pieces and placing them together to create multiple systems, they are interchangeable and replaceable. Decomposability means when the individual parts of a system cannot be broken down any further. Emergence is when components of a system do things together that they would not be able to do on their own. Chaos Theory is a complex system that is very susceptible to change and minor changes can lead to severe consequences. Tessellation is a mosaic pattern that draws in the viewers of the piece. I have seen tessellation patterns in clothing and other decorative objects such as curtains and rugs. Pineapples The difference is that modularity does not all have to have the same shape as the tessellations do. Modularity systems can be simpler. The difference is whether or not it is creating a pattern. If the artpiece is creating a repetitive pattern it is uniform or periodic, but then it is not following any pattern it is complexity or aperiodic.
